1 Course Overview

Week Earth system focus Modelling focus
1 Building a conceptual Earth System Model From diagrams to equations: reservoirs, fluxes, state variables, prognostic vs. diagnostic variables, conservation, numerical integration
2 The atmosphere: Earth’s energy balance Time scales, forcing and response, equilibrium vs. transient response, spin-up, climate sensitivity
3 Ocean circulation Dynamical systems: equilibria, stability, Jacobian, eigenvalues, phase portraits
4 Ocean circulation under freshwater forcing Nonlinear systems, feedbacks, multiple equilibria, hysteresis, tipping points
5 Ocean carbon chemistry Coupling components, flux exchange, conservation in coupled models, tracers
6 Marine biology Nonlinear interactions, emergent behaviour, oscillations, feedbacks, increasing model complexity
7 Land carbon cycle Parameters, uncertainty, sensitivity analysis, parameter space exploration
8 Cryosphere Threshold behaviour, positive feedbacks, slow components, timescale separation
9 Coupling the Earth System Model architecture, modularity, component coupling, numerical stability, coupled-model spin-up
10 Future climate experiments Experimental design, scenarios, ensembles, uncertainty propagation, global sensitivity analysis
11 Learning from observations Model evaluation, calibration, parameter optimization, inverse modelling
12 Student modelling investigations Models as scientific experiments: hypothesis testing, interpretation, communication
